The Complete Guide to ICT Order Blocks in 2025

David Okafor · Senior FX Analyst
January 15, 2025 8 min read 4,281 views

Order blocks are one of the most powerful concepts in the Inner Circle Trader (ICT) methodology and Smart Money Concepts (SMC). When identified correctly, they give you a precise, high-probability area to enter trades with minimal risk and maximum reward potential.

What is an Order Block?

An order block is the last candle before an impulsive move that displaces price significantly. It represents a zone where institutional traders (banks, hedge funds, large operators) placed significant buy or sell orders — orders so large that they couldn't be filled all at once.

When price returns to this zone, those same institutions are waiting with their remaining orders. This creates a magnet effect that makes order blocks self-fulfilling as price consistently reacts at these levels.

Types of Order Blocks

Bullish Order Block (BOB): The last bearish (red) candle before a significant bullish displacement. When price returns to this zone, we look for buy entries.

Bearish Order Block (BEOB): The last bullish (green) candle before a significant bearish displacement. When price returns to this zone, we look for sell entries.

Pro Tip: The most powerful order blocks are found on higher timeframes (4H, Daily, Weekly) and confirmed with a Fair Value Gap (FVG) or Breaker Block structure nearby.

How to Identify a Valid Order Block

Not every candle before a move is a valid order block. Here are the criteria we use at Forex Academy Pro:

  • 1. Significant Displacement: The move after the OB must be impulsive, not gradual. Multiple candles of the same colour with large bodies.
  • 2. Break of Structure (BOS): The displacement must break a recent swing high or low, confirming institutional intent.
  • 3. Volume Confirmation: Higher-than-average volume at the formation point adds confluence.
  • 4. Premium/Discount Zones: Bullish OBs in discount (below equilibrium) and bearish OBs in premium (above equilibrium) carry higher probability.

Entry, Stop Loss and Take Profit

Once price returns to a valid OB zone:

  • Entry: Enter at the 50% level of the OB candle (the "CE" — consequent encroachment)
  • Stop Loss: 5–10 pips below the low of the OB (for bullish), above the high (for bearish)
  • Take Profit: Target the previous swing high/low, or the 2R level minimum
